The Keeper of Time
Elias was the last watchmaker in a town that had forgotten the value of ticking seconds. His shop was small, crammed with clocks that hummed a chaotic symphony of passing time. People preferred digital screens now, but once a year, a young girl named Maya would visit, fascinated by the brass gears.
Elias looked at her, his eyes twinkling behind thick glasses. He pointed to a small, non-ticking pocket watch on his desk. "I wouldn't stop it, child. I would just slow it down, right now, in this moment—talking to you."
Years later, Maya returned to the shop, now a woman. The shop was empty, but on the desk lay the same brass pocket watch. It was ticking steadily.
